# Power Earnings Gap (PEG)

> **Stocks that gap up 5%+ on earnings with 2x average volume produce a 60-68% win rate over 5-15 day holds, driven by institutional repositioning into the new price level.**

## Overview
- **Author/Origin:** Dan Zanger, Mark Minervini
- **Family:** Momentum
- **Direction:** Long
- **Best Market Regime:** Trending Up
- **Typical Hold Time:** 5-15 days

## Why It Works

A large earnings gap accompanied by massive volume represents something fundamentally different from a normal price move. When a company reports earnings that exceed expectations by a wide margin, institutional investors -- mutual funds, pension funds, hedge funds -- must rapidly establish or increase their positions. These funds cannot accumulate shares gradually as they would during a quiet basing period. The gap forces them to buy aggressively at the new price level, and their sheer buying pressure establishes a floor.

This is why power earnings gaps so often refuse to fill. The gap day's low becomes a line in the sand. Institutions that bought on gap day have a vested interest in defending that price. If the stock drifts back toward the gap, these same buyers step in again, creating a self-reinforcing support zone. Dan Zanger, who turned $10,775 into $18 million during the late-1990s tech rally, built much of his approach around identifying these institutional footprints.

Mark Minervini, two-time U.S. Investing Champion, has emphasized that earnings gaps are one of the few events that can genuinely change a stock's trajectory overnight. The key filter is the 3-day hold test: if the stock does not fill the gap within 3 trading days, institutional commitment is confirmed. "The biggest winners almost always emerge from a powerful earnings gap that holds and builds a new base," Minervini writes in *Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard* (2013). Without the volume confirmation and the hold test, a gap is just noise.

## Entry Rules

1. Stock gaps up 5% or more on an earnings announcement
2. Gap-day volume is at least 2x the 50-day average volume
3. Gap holds above prior resistance for 3+ consecutive trading days (the hold test)
4. The gap does not fill -- price stays above the pre-gap close
5. Relative Strength (RS) rank is above 60 versus the broader market
6. The stock's sector is showing relative strength versus the S&P 500
7. Price is above the 50-day simple moving average
8. The gap is not excessively extended (gap range should be under 20% to avoid chasing)
9. ADX(14) above 20, confirming trend strength rather than range-bound drift

## Exit Framework

### Stop Loss
Place the stop below the low of the gap day. This is the institutional defense line -- if price breaks below it, the thesis is invalidated and funds are likely distributing rather than accumulating.

### Target 1 (Scale Out)
1.5x the gap range measured from the gap-day close. Scale out 50% of the position at this level to lock in gains while the setup is working.

### Target 2 (Remainder)
3.0x the gap range from the gap-day close. The remaining 50% rides for the full measured move, which captures the follow-through buying that typically emerges after the initial gap holds.

### Trailing Stop
Once Target 1 is reached, trail the stop below the most recent swing low on the daily chart. This allows the trade to breathe through normal pullbacks while protecting against a reversal.

## Common Mistakes

- **Chasing gaps that are too extended.** A 25% gap on earnings is exciting but leaves very little room for a favorable risk/reward entry. Stick to 5-20% gap ranges.
- **Buying gaps on weak volume.** A gap without institutional volume (below 2x average) is often driven by retail speculation and has a much higher fill rate.
- **Ignoring sector context.** Even a strong earnings report will struggle if the entire sector is rotating out. Confirm sector relative strength before entering.
- **Not waiting for the 3-day hold test.** Buying on gap day feels urgent, but roughly 30-40% of earnings gaps fill within the first three sessions. The hold test filters these out.
- **Trading earnings gaps in bear markets.** Even genuinely strong gaps get sold into during Stage 4 declines. Power earnings gaps work best when the broad market is in an uptrend.
